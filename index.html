Experience a wholly different way of life in this day-long excursion to meet the Buddhist Nuns of Mandalay. After a scenic drive over the Irrawaddy river, arrive at the fascinating Sagaing Buddhist Nunnery. This spiritual place is home to more than 6,000 Buddhist nuns who dress in wonderfully pastel pink robes. Follow their daily schedule, learn about their lives and deepen your understanding of Buddhism before taking part in a 30-minute guided meditation. Enjoy a home-made vegetarian lunch and have the chance to explore the well-maintained complex. A fascinating way to learn about the culture and religion of this mesmerising place.

Tour Highlights

  • Have the opportunity to meet Burmese Buddhist Nuns
  • Dine on a delicious vegetarian meal made by the Nuns
  • Hear about their Buddhist studies and take part in a guided meditation practice

Inclusions

  • Transfers by air-conditioned vehicle

DAY 1 - A day with the Buddhist Nuns of Mandalay

This fascinating full-day excursion provides the unique opportunity to meet with the enchanting Buddhist nuns of Mandalay. Begin with an hour-long drive from the city, across the Irrawaddy River to reach the tranquil Sagaing Buddhist Nunnery. Upon arrival meet with the sanguine Abbess of the nunnery and hear about life in this captivating place. Talk to the nuns and marvel at the spectacle of hundreds of shaven-headed nuns dressed in bubble-gum pink and orange robes.

From the Abbess to the Thilashin (or novice nuns), these women are amongst the most highly educated nuns in the world. Listen as they describe their studies of Buddhist literature and have the chance to ask questions about their daily lives.

Take a stroll through the gloriously picturesque gardens and admire the beautiful and unique lacquerware Buddha before taking part in a 30-minute meditation session guided by the nuns themselves.

Next, enjoy lunch with every single resident in this sprawling complex. As is customary in Buddhist communities, the food is donated by the local community and cooked by the nuns themselves. Watch as hundreds of rows of shoes are laid neatly next to one another outside the huge communal dining area before enjoying a delicious meal.

This fascinating day will give visitors the chance to see a totally different way of life.
